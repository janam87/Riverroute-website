Proposed Section Structure

Consolidated from 7 sections down to 5. Each one bold and purposeful.

Proposed Flow

A

Option A — Story-Led (CRED-closest)

1. Hero — Full-screen black. One massive headline. One line of subtext. No buttons above the fold — let the text breathe.

2. The Industry — Big stat counter (₹2.5L Cr) with a bold statement about M&E. Verticals shown as a minimal grid, not cards. Short, punchy copy.

3. What We're Building — Merge "Building" + "Who We Serve". Three product horizons with bold headlines. Audiences woven into each horizon as who benefits. Waitlist CTA at the end.

4. The People — Founders section, cinematic style. Large photos (when ready), minimal bio text, credentials as subtle tags.

5. Footer — Contact form simplified to just email capture (waitlist-focused). Links, legal, done.

B

Option B — Impact-Led

1. Hero — Full-screen. Massive headline. Single CTA button.

2. The Problem — Bold statement about the broken M&E industry. Counter stat. No cards — just typography and whitespace doing the work.

3. The Solution — Merge "Building" + "Who We Serve" + "Industry verticals". Three bold product statements. Minimal supporting text.

4. The Founders — Keep but cinematic. Photos + one-liner each.

5. Get In — Contact/waitlist as a bold call-to-action section, not a form-heavy page. Email + name only. Footer integrated below.

C

Option C — Minimal (Most Aggressive Cut)

1. Hero — Full-screen. Just the headline. Nothing else.

2. The Story — One scrolling section that tells everything: industry problem → what you're building → who it's for. CRED-style large text paragraphs that scroll into view, word by word.

3. Get In — Waitlist capture. Founders mentioned as a line, not a section. Footer below.

Just 3 sections. Maximum cinema. Maximum confidence.
